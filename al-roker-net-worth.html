Al Roker | Career

Al Roker started his career in 1976 as the weather anchor at an independent station named WTTG-TV.

At first, he took the help of gimmicky to forecast the weather. He stopped doing it after Willard Scott (Al’s favorite anchor of NBC) asked him to be himself.

In 1978, Al moved to  Cleveland and worked with NBC affiliate WKYC-TV. After working for five years there, he moved back to New York.

In 1996, he became the new face of the Today Show and became the weatherman and anchor.

The famous Al has been caught in crazy weather conditions throughout his career; some of the interesting conditions have been captured on camera.

If there’s one occasion that stands out, it’s when Roker was covering Hurricane Wilma in 2005.

Also, Al started forecasting for many radios. His program Wake Up With Al aired from 2009 to 2015.

Similarly, from 2012 to 2017, he hosted games shows like Game On TODAY on NBC.

Despite finding considerable success, Al never left the job of the Today Show. He claims that he loves being there, and still, he hosts it every day.
