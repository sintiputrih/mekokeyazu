Who Is Gabriel Bonfim? UFC Fighter: Wiki, Family And Nationality

Publish date: 2024-05-14

Gabriel Gabriel Bonfim is a professional mixed martial artist who competes in the UFC welterweight division. His supporters and opponents perceive the pro-MMA fighter as an unbeatable martial artist. Despite the dearth of information on the 26-year-old’s schooling, reports indicate he is a college graduate. Marretinha made his official MMA debut on December 13, 2014, when he faced Santos in his first professional bout.

In the second round, Bonfim defeated Santos by submitting him with a triangle choke. Furthermore, his record of 15 victories and one defeat, demonstrating his unblemished position in the sport, definitely distinguishes him. Bonfim trains hard at Cerrado MMA in Brazil to keep his competitive edge. Gabriel, who is 6’1″, is known for his exceptional hitting ability, which has become his trademark.

Gabriel Bonfim Wiki: Family and Nationality

Gabriel “Marretinha” Bonfim was born on August 20, 1997, in Braslia, Distrito Federal, Brazil. Bonfim is a Brazilian citizen of mixed origin who practices Christianity. He grew up raised in Brazil and began learning martial arts there, eventually earning a black belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu.

Gabriel also grew up with his brother Ismael Bonfim. Ismael, like Gabriel, is a professional MMA fighter who competes in the highly competitive lightweight class. The Bonfim brothers have been training together since infancy and are both outstanding fighters in the MMA world. The MMA fighter is skilled at maintaining his privacy, as seen by his refusal to provide information about his parents. On Instagram, which is often filled with personal discoveries, he selectively concentrates on highlighting his professional accomplishments, allowing followers and the media to guess about his relationship status.

Bonfim, the reigning MMA champion, has not only won the octagon but also acquired a $500k net worth. This financial achievement attests to his great path in the world of MMA as well as his rising prominence as a sports figure.

Bonfim Loss: UFC Sao Paulo

Gabriel has been recognized as an unbeaten powerhouse in the professional MMA scene up until the previous UFC event in Sao Paulo, with a spotless record of 15 wins and 0 defeats as of November 4. However, the story took an unexpected turn when Nicolas Dalby staged a miraculous comeback, defeating the supposedly unstoppable Bonfim in the second round by TKO. Gabriel’s first professional defeat sent shockwaves across his passionate support base.

Following the game, a disgruntled audience of Bonfim fans voiced shock at seeing their unbeaten hero collapse. In reaction to the outraged supporters, Dalby said that Gabriel’s abilities had been overestimated. He recognized Gabriel’s aptitude but claimed that he had not yet been thoroughly examined. According to Dalby, his expertise was critical in achieving the victory. While understanding Gabriel’s appreciation for Bonfim’s talents, he thought there was more for Gabriel to learn, noting his study of a couple of Bonfim’s battles before their confrontation.

The match’s aftermath elicited a wide range of responses. While some fans disregarded it as a game, others were perplexed. Gabriel’s immaculate record was changed from 15 wins and 0 losses to 15 wins and one defeat, indicating a substantial reversal in his career trajectory.
