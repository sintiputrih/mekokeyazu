NFL World Shell-Shocked as Ex-Colts Lineman Arrested for Two Heinous Crimes

Publish date: 2024-05-01

NFL players often find it hard to escape trouble and the limelight, even when they retire from the game. Former Indianapolis Colts offensive lineman Joe Staysniak has landed himself in a significant amount of it after authorities levied multiple charges against him owing to a domestic violence incident. But what did he do, so much so, that the police booked him?

Cops arrested Staysniak on Tuesday after a heated situation involving his son, Lucas Staysniak, and his son’s acquaintance. The Hendricks County police came to the scene after a “delayed domestic” incident, resulting in a registered affidavit against the former NFL athlete and radio host.

Joe Staysniak ends up in a domestic dispute

The San Diego Chargers and Indianapolis pass rush blocker faces strangulation and battery charges after he attempted to choke his son’s alleged boyfriend and also punched his lips bloody. After that, Staysniak proceeded to pull a firearm and threaten who he claims he thought were trespassers on his property.

According to Hendrick County’s jail records, the cops brought Joe into the Sherriff’s department at 1:40 am on Tuesday morning. The charges against him included battery, firearm pointing, strangulation, intimidation, and domestic battery. Lucas Staysniak informed the police that he was sitting with his friend in a parked car on the premises when Joe started the ruckus.

“His [Lucas’] father opened the rear right passenger door and that when he did this, he grabbed [passenger’s] hood and was choking him. Lucas stated that his father then punched him, causing his lip to bleed and then punched [passenger]. He stated that his father then displayed a firearm and told him and [passenger] that they were lucky he saw who it was,” the official probable cause affidavit said.

Fans assumed a homophobia angle but Joe cleared all doubts

Joe Staysniak received a call from his neighbors about a suspicious vehicle parked in front of his property. And Joe assumed the worst, leading to the altercation with his son and his friend.
