Jose Mourinho: Career

Career as a player

Jose Mourinho wanted to be a professional football player like his father and decided to join the Belenenses Youth team in 1988

Further, he was signed by the Portuguese Club Rio Ave in 1980.

Evidently, Mourinho was an attacking midfielder with the playing style of a creative and box-to-box midfielder.

Nevertheless, he then transfers to the CF Belenenses in 1982, where he scored 2 goals in 16 appearances.

Unfortunately, Jose Mourinho had to join the low-tier Portuguese club Sesimbra as his former club decided to terminated his contract in 1983.

Sadly, he started to feel disgusted about his poor performances and lack of playing time.

However, The midfielder decided to give it a last shot and joined Comercio e Industria in 1985, where he played for two seasons.

Finally, Jose Mourinho announced his retirement from his professional football player career in 1987 with a total of 94 club appearances and 13 goals scored.
