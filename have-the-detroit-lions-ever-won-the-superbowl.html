Have the Detroit Lions Ever won the Superbowl?

Publish date: 2024-07-18

The Detroit Lions have never won the Super Bowl but have won the National Football League (NFL) championship four times before the 1970 AFL-NFL merger. These victories occurred in 1935, 1952, 1953, and 1957, and the team has a playoff record of 7 wins and 13 losses. Despite their lack of Super Bowl success, the Lions are considered a historic team in the NFL, with a loyal fan base and a significant impact on the league's history.

Detroit Lions' Championship History

The Lions' NFL championship victories took place before the Super Bowl era. They won their titles in the following years:

  • 1935: Defeated the New York Giants with a score of 26-7 at the University of Detroit Stadium in Detroit, Michigan.
  • 1952: Defeated the Cleveland Browns with a score of 17-7 at the Cleveland Municipal Stadium in Cleveland, Ohio.
  • 1953: Defeated the Cleveland Browns with a score of 17-16 at Briggs Stadium in Detroit.
  • 1957: Defeated the Cleveland Browns with a score of 59-14 at Briggs Stadium in Detroit.

The Detroit Lions have never reached the Super Bowl, making them the only NFC team to have never made an appearance in the Super Bowl. However, they are not alone in this regard, as three other NFL teams, including the Cleveland Browns, Jacksonville Jaguars, and Houston Texans, have also never played in the Super Bowl, all belonging to the AFC.
