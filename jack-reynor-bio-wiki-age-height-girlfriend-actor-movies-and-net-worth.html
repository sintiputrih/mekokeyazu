Jack Reynor Biography

Jack Reynor is an Irish actor who was born in Longmont, Colorado, United States. He co-starred opposite Florence Pugh in A24’s breakout summer 2019 hit, Midsommar, directed by Ari Aster. His well-known roles include the lead in Lenny Abrahamson’s film What Richard Did(2012) which earned him the IFTA Award for Best Film Actor. He is also known for the Transformers: Age of Extinction(2014)

Jack Actor

In early 2010, Jack appeared as Robbie, the boy next door, in the live format, unscripted Dollhouse, directed by Kirsten Sheridan, which premiered at Berlinale 2012. He played the role of Richard Karlsen in Lenny Abrahamson’s feature What Richard Did, which screened at the 2013 Tribeca Film Festival. The film depicts the fall of a high school rugby star and a golden boy whose world unravels after his involvement in an act of careless violence

Jack appeared as Shane, an Irish race car driver, in Transformers: Age of Extinction, the fourth film in the Transformers series in January 2013. The film was released in June 2014 and co-starred Mark Wahlberg and Nicola Peltz. Jack starred in upcoming writer/director Gerard Barrett’s film Glassland with Toni Collette and Will Poulter, in which he portrays a young taxi driver who struggles to instill a sense of structure in his life while his mother suffers from severe alcoholism

Jack starred with Michael Fassbender, Marion Cotillard, and David Thewlis as Malcolm Canmore, the rightful heir to the throne, in The Weinstein Company’s feature film Macbeth, directed by Justin Kurzel, and premiered in Edinburgh in September 2015. Jack made an appearance in the 2017 mini-series Philip K. Dick’s Electric Dreams and the 2018 drama film On the Basis of Sex and then starred in the 2019 horror film Midsommar alongside Florence Pugh. He will appear in the upcoming Amazon Prime Video series The Peripheral.
