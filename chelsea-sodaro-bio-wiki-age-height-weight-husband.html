Chelsea Sodaro Ironman World Champs 2022

The 33-year-old mom, who gave birth to daughter Skylar just 18 months prior, won the race with a time of 8 hours, 33 minutes, and 46 seconds in Kona. Sodaro, who had never made it to Ironman World Championships until this year, told NBC Sports, “My mind is a little bit blown right now,” after her win.

“This is the culmination of things being right in my life and having perspective. This is freakin’ incredible, but the greatest gift at the end of the finish line is my little 18-month-old.”

Sodaro beat out Swiss competitor, and five-time Ironman title winner, Daniela Ryf, who came in second. She also defeated British athlete and third-place finisher Lucy Charles-Barclay by 7 minutes and 50 seconds.

Chelsea Sodaro Career

Chelsea Sodaro is a professional runner who has won two national titles, the USA 10km road championships in 2012 and the 3000m race at 2013 USATF Indoor Championships. Sodaro was coached by fellow Cal alum and Olympian Magdalena Boulet before she transitioned to triathlon in 2017.

In October 2022, Sodaro won the Ironman World Championship in Kona, Hawaii, becoming the first Kona novice champion since Chrissie Wellington and the first American woman to do so since 1996. It was her second professional distance triathlon.

As a professional runner, Sodaro won two national titles. She won the USA 10 km road championships in 2012, in her debut at that distance. At 2013 USATF Indoor Championships, she won the 3000m race, edging out Emily Infeld. She was coached by fellow Cal alum and Olympian Magdalena Boulet.
