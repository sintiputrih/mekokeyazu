Josh Warrington Wife: Is Josh Warrington Married? – Between 2013 and 2017, Josh Warrington held the British, Commonwealth, European, and WBC International featherweight titles.

‘Fighting for a City,’ a documentary-style film shot over two years, premiered in 2018.

Josh Warrington was expelled from John Smeaton Academy after failing to achieve the required grades to continue his education. On the advice of his father, Sean O’Hagan, he retook his exams. He later attended the University of Leeds and graduated in 2013.

He is a Leeds United and Leeds Rhinos fan. In the early stages of his career, Warrington was signed to Eddie Hearn’s Matchroom Boxing. In 2016, he left Matchroom to join Frank Warren’s Queensberry Promotions, before returning to Matchroom in February 2020.

Sean O’Hagan, his father, trains him. Steve Wood of VIP Boxing manages him, as does former WBO lightweight world champion Terry Flanagan.

Warrington made his professional debut in October 2009, defeating Wolverhampton’s Delroy Spencer (11-89-3) before going on to defeat Latvia’s Pavels Senkovs (2-10-2), both by 40-36 points decision.

Warrington won all three of his fights in 2010, defeating Danny McDermid (0-1), John Riley (0-0), and Youssef Al Hamidi (7-27-2), all by points decision.

By the end of 2011, Warrington had kept his unbeaten record going with points victories over Steve Gethin (11-56-3), Chris Riley (3-3-2), Marc Callaghan (19-21-1), Dougie Curran (5-8-1), and Ian Bailey (7-8).

Warrington won points decisions over Dan Naylor (1-8) and Ibrar Riyaz (4-34-1) in the first half of 2012.

Warrington won his first professional title in November when he won the vacant English featherweight title by unanimous decision over previously unbeaten Dudley-born prospect Chris Male (11-0). On November 9, the fight took place at the Venue in Dudley.
