Are Jessica Neal And James Martin Still Married?

Yes, James Martin and Jessica Neal are still married as of 2022. It seems that the duo has overcome their financial problem which was straining their relationship.

In case you missed their drama, James wasn’t taking home a paycheck. So, Jessica felt pressured to be the sole breadwinner working two jobs. Moreover, despite being overworked, Jessica also refused to relinquish control of her responsibilities at the restaurant to pay extra money for help. And this wasn’t sitting well with James.

Looking back, these restaurateurs got married on August 30, 2014, eight months after their engagement. Since then, the duo has come across several obstacles but they’ve always pulled it through. No wonder, Jessica was extremely grateful for her husband; especially for his patience, love, and faith.

“I wouldn’t be who I am today without him (and the many loving friends and family that have helped guide and support us along the way),” Jessica posted on her IG on their 5th anniversary.

James Martin Career

James Martin is a chef through and through. He has had over a decade of experience in fine dining. Starting his journey at the Art Institute of Washington’s culinary program while working for Chef RJ Cooper at Vidalia (2007 – 2008), James then moved to Chicago and worked under Chef Bruce Sherman at Northpond for 7 months.

In 2009, James then started as “Chef de Partie” at Michael Mina’s Bourbon Steak at Four Seasons and continued developing his skills as a cook at three Michelin Star Chef Jean Georges’ flagship restaurant in New York City, before returning to the area to join the Wine Kitchen (2011 – 2012) in Frederick, MD as “Executive Sous Chef.”

Then on, James served as an “Executive Chef” at Restaurant Nora, District Supper, Pamplona, A10 restaurant, and Four Corners. And today, he owns his own restaurant Bocadillo Market which has been nominated for Jean Banchet Awards for Culinary Excellence.

His restaurant explored the culinary traditions behind the cuisine, from the Moor’s influence on Spanish agriculture and dishes to the Spanish impact on low country food and agriculture. It served Coffee, Sweets, Bocadillos, Dinner, Brunch, Wine, and Cocktails. Also, they were open for reservations.

As for James’s education, James graduated from Charles Herbert Flowers High School in 2005 and went on to study a 2-year Culinary Arts course at the Art Institute of Washington.

Trivia: Bocadillo Market was closed from July 3rd through July 12th for “some much-needed rest.”
